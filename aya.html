About

I'm a physician-scientist and Research Fellow at Mass General Hospital's Metabolism Unit, where I study cardiovascular risk in people with HIV through the REPRIEVE Trial. My work bridges clinical medicine, biostatistics, and AI—applying advanced statistical modeling, machine learning, and bioinformatics to translational research questions. I hold an MMSc from Harvard Medical School (awarded the Gordon Williams Gold Medal) and an MD from Technion Institute of Technology. I'm passionate about leveraging data science to improve patient outcomes and mentor the next generation of clinical researchers.
